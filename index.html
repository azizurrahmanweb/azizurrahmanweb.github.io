About the Lab

Bridging theoretical foundations with real-world statistical applications

Our Mission

TASiLab (Theoretical and Applied Statistical Innovation Lab) is dedicated to advancing statistical science through rigorous theoretical development and innovative applications. We focus on creating novel methodologies that address complex challenges in public health, biostatistics, and data science.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines traditional statistical theory with modern computational techniques, including machine learning and functional data analysis, to solve pressing problems in healthcare, epidemiology, and social sciences.

Research Philosophy

We believe in the power of data-driven insights to transform decision-making. Our research emphasizes reproducibility, methodological rigor, and practical applicability to real-world problems.

Research Areas

Exploring cutting-edge statistical methodologies across multiple domains

01

Functional Data Analysis

Developing methods for analyzing curves, surfaces, and other infinite-dimensional data objects with applications in mortality forecasting and climate pattern analysis.

02

Biostatistics & Public Health

Applying statistical methods to understand health outcomes, disease patterns, and healthcare interventions in developing countries with focus on maternal and child health.

03

Machine Learning in Healthcare

Integrating modern ML algorithms with traditional statistical approaches for predictive modeling, including infant mortality prediction and disease classification.

04

Time Series & Forecasting

Advanced forecasting methods including ARIMA, neural networks, and hybrid approaches for epidemiological and demographic time series data.

05

Bayesian Modeling

Bayesian statistical approaches for complex hierarchical models, spatial analysis, and uncertainty quantification in health and social science research.

06

Multi-state Markov Models

Modeling disease progression and state transitions with applications in chronic disease research and survival analysis.
