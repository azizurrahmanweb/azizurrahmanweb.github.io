About TASiLab

Theoretical and Applied Statistical Innovation Laboratory

Mission

TASiLab (Theoretical and Applied Statistical Innovation Lab) is a research group dedicated to developing novel statistical methodologies that bridge the gap between rigorous theoretical foundations and practical real-world applications. Founded by Azizur Rahman, the lab focuses on advancing statistical science in biostatistics, public health, and data science.

Research Philosophy

We believe that impactful statistical research must be both theoretically sound and practically applicable. Our work emphasizes:

  • Methodological Rigor: Developing statistically principled approaches with strong theoretical foundations
  • Computational Efficiency: Creating fast algorithms suitable for large-scale health data
  • Real-World Impact: Applying methods to solve pressing public health challenges
  • Reproducibility: Ensuring all research is transparent and reproducible

Core Research Areas

Our primary research thrust is in fast approximate inference using variational Bayes methods for intensive longitudinal data. We also work on functional data analysis, machine learning for health outcomes, and multi-state Markov models.

Background and education

Biography

Azizur Rahman is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Community Health Sciences at the University of Manitoba, working under Dr. Depeng Jiang. His doctoral research focuses on fast approximate inference for multilevel threshold autoregressive models using variational Bayes.

He is on study leave from Associate Professor at Statistics, Jahangirnagar University, Bangladesh (faculty since 2014). He also serves as Program Evaluator at the Social Innovation Office, Government of Manitoba.
