About TASiLab

Theoretical and Applied Statistical Innovation Laboratory

Mission

The TASi Lab is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of statistical science by integrating rigorous theoretical foundations with cutting-edge applied methodologies. Our mission is to develop innovative statistical models and computational tools that address complex, real-world challenges across diverse domains. We strive to foster collaboration, promote reproducible research, and empower decision-making through robust, data-driven insights.

Vision

To be a global leader in statistical innovation, shaping the future of data science and analytics through pioneering research and transformative applications. We envision a world where theoretical breakthroughs seamlessly translate into practical solutions, driving progress in science, technology, and society.

About the Director

Background and education

Biography

Azizur Rahman is a PhD Candidate (ABD) in Biostatistics at the Department of Community Health Sciences, University of Manitoba, working under Dr. Depeng Jiang. His thesis focuses on fast approximate Bayesian estimation algorithms for discrete and continuous time models in intensive longitudinal data. Expected graduation: June 2026.

He is on study leave from Associate Professor at Statistics and Data Science, Jahangirnagar University, Bangladesh. He serves as Program Evaluator at the Social Innovation Office, Government of Manitoba.

Research Areas

Statistical methods at the intersection of theory and application

01

Intensive Longitudinal Data

Fast approximate Bayesian estimation using variational inference for TAR and Hidden Markov models.

02

Functional Data Analysis

FDA methods for mortality forecasting, influenza patterns, climate, and partial functional data.

03

Machine Learning in Healthcare

Interpretable ML for infant mortality, Alzheimer's prediction, and ECG-based AF detection.

04

Bayesian Modeling

Bayesian logistic regression, change point analysis, hierarchical models for public health.

05

Multi-state Markov Models

Disease progression, air pollution dynamics, blood pressure transitions in diabetic patients.

06

Public Health Epidemiology

Maternal/child health, HIV testing behavior, nutritional status, erosion trajectory modeling.
